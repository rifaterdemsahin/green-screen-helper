🎥 Camera Focus Guide

Excellent question — this is one of the biggest challenges in a one-person online course production setup. Getting the focus right when you don’t have a camera operator can make or break your video quality. Let’s go through the best, practical, one-person focus strategies, depending on your setup.

1. If You’re Using a DSLR / Mirrorless Camera

Here’s the ideal solo workflow:

✅ Option A: Face/eye autofocus (best if your camera supports it)

  • Turn on face/eye detection AF (on Canon, Sony, Nikon, Panasonic, etc.).
  • Set the AF mode to continuous (AF-C).
  • Use touch tracking if available — tap your face on the flip screen.
  • Keep a bit of distance (1.2–2m) so small movements don’t push the lens to refocus constantly.
  • Use a wide but not too shallow aperture (f/2.8–f/4) for a natural blur but some margin for movement.

✅ Option B: Manual focus lock (safer if AF hunts)

If your autofocus hunts (focus goes in/out), do this:

  1. Place a stand-in object (e.g., light stand, broom handle, or chair) exactly where you’ll stand/sit.
  2. Focus manually on that object.
  3. Switch focus to manual mode to lock it.
  4. Step into the spot and start recording.
  5. Use a monitor or app to double-check focus (see below).

2. Monitor Yourself While Filming

You can preview and control focus remotely:

Using a mobile app (Wi-Fi / Bluetooth)

  • Canon: Camera Connect
  • Sony: Imaging Edge Mobile
  • Nikon: SnapBridge
  • Panasonic: Lumix Sync

You can view live focus, adjust it, and start/stop recording.

Using an external field monitor

  • A 5–7” HDMI monitor (like Feelworld, SmallHD, or Atomos) lets you see if you’re sharp.
  • Mount it near your camera or slightly under your lens.

3. Set up markers for consistent focus

Place tape marks on the floor for:

  • Your standing/sitting position.
  • Focus distance (so you always hit your sharp spot).

4. Use focus assist tools

If your camera or monitor supports it:

  • Focus peaking: highlights edges that are sharp.
  • Magnify view: zoom in temporarily to fine-tune focus before recording.

5. Lens and settings tips

  • Use a prime lens around 24–35mm (APS-C) or 35–50mm (full frame).
  • Aperture: f/2.8–f/4 for shallow-but-safe depth of field.
  • Avoid shooting wide open (like f/1.4 or f/1.8) unless you’re extremely still.
  • Keep subject–background distance wide for better separation.

🧠 Pro Workflow Summary (for solo course creators)

Step Action
1 Mark your standing/sitting spot
2 Place stand-in object for pre-focus
3 Focus manually or set face-tracking AF
4 Check with monitor or camera app
5 Lock exposure + white balance
6 Start rolling and speak naturally
