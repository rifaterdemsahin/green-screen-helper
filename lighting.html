📘 3-Light Green Screen Setup Theory

KEY Key Light

The main illuminating source for your face/body. Set it at 45 degrees relative to the camera axis and elevated about 45 degrees pointing down. This provides depth and shapes your features.

FILL Fill Light

Placed opposite the key light, usually at a lower intensity (e.g. 50% or 1:2 ratio). It prevents heavy shadows on the opposite side of your face. For small spaces, use a soft diffuser to avoid bouncing harsh light off side walls.

RIM Back/Rim Light

Placed behind you, angled down at your shoulders and hair. It creates a subtle glowing border that separates you from the green background and neutralizes green light reflections (spill) landing on you.

⚠️ Compact Room Rules (2.0m x 4.0m)

  • Spill Hazard: Because the room length is only 4.0m, placing the camera at 3.6m and subject at 1.6m gives you 1.6m separation from the green screen. This is the sweet spot. Moving closer than 1.2m causes the subject's key light to cast shadows on the screen, and green color will reflect onto the subject's shoulders.
  • Reflection Bouncing: The room is only 2 meters wide. Lights placed too close to side walls will bounce off the wall and ruin the camera angle coverage. Use flag shields or grid diffusers on key/fill lights.
  • Angle Calculation: With the camera at 3.6m and subject at 1.6m, the camera-to-subject distance is 2.0 meters. A standard focal length of 24mm to 35mm (full-frame equivalent) is ideal.

📸 Sony ZV-1 Best Practice Guidelines

  • Lens Zoom & Framing: The Sony ZV-1 features a 1-inch sensor and a 24-70mm equivalent lens. At a distance of 2.0m from the subject, shooting at the widest setting (24mm equivalent / actual 9.4mm) will capture beyond the edges of the green screen in a 2m wide room. Zoom in slightly to around 15mm (approx. 40mm equivalent) to narrow your camera's field of view to match the green screen width perfectly.
  • Aperture (Depth of Field): Shoot at f/2.8 (or wide open). This creates a shallow depth of field, blurring any folds or wrinkles on the green screen background, which makes chroma keying significantly easier.
  • Focusing: Switch to Manual Focus (MF) and lock the focus distance to 2.0m. Disable Auto-Focus to prevent the camera lens from hunting or focusing on the green background screen.
  • Exposure & Picture Profile: Set ISO to 125 (lowest native ISO). Use Picture Profile PP8 (S-Log2 / S-Gamut3.Cine) for maximum dynamic range, or choose standard profile with details and contrast slightly reduced for clean keying without grading.
  • Background Quality Wash (Highly Recommended): A 3-light setup is great for subject illumination, but adding a **dedicated green screen wash light** will elevate keying quality to a professional level. Illuminating the background evenly removes shadows, eliminates wrinkles, and facilitates a perfect chroma key in post-production.
