Showing Example Setup Report

To test your own environment, upload a setup picture on the left. The report below illustrates common issues diagnosed from a sample setup with uneven lighting and framing errors.

1. Harsh, Uneven Lighting & Shadows

The Problem: The most significant issue is the prominent, angular shadow/color shift cutting right through the middle of the screen.

Why it matters: For a green screen to work perfectly, the green needs to be a completely uniform, flat color. The sharp contrast between the bright green on the left/top and the darker, shadowed green in the center means your editing software will struggle. If you key out the bright green, the dark green shadow might remain, or vice versa, leading to a "fuzzy" or artifact-heavy edge around your subject.

2. Framing and Edge Obstructions

  • Top-Right Corner: There is a piece of black material (likely a clamp, part of the frame, or a softbox) encroaching onto the green area.
  • Right Edge: A strip of the background wall or stand is visible.

Why it matters: While you can fix some of this with a "garbage matte" (cropping out the edges in editing), it severely limits your subject's movement and forces you to do extra manual work in post.

💡 Quick Tips to Fix This:

⚡ Light the Screen Separately

Try using two dedicated lights placed on either side of the green screen at 45-degree angles to wash it evenly with light, completely erasing that central shadow.

🚶 Step Away from the Screen

Make sure your subject stands at least 4–6 feet in front of the screen. This prevents their body from casting new shadows onto the backdrop.

⚙️ Adjust Your Camera Angle

Zoom in slightly or adjust the camera position to fill the frame entirely with green, eliminating the black artifact in the top right and the white gap on the side.
